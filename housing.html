1 Scheme For Working Women Hostel A scheme to promote accommodation for working women, with day care facility for their children, wherever possible, in urban, semi urban, or even rural areas where employment opportunity for women exist. ● Safe and secure Hostel Facilities to working women or women under going job Training
● Girls up to the age of 18 years and boys up to the age of 5 years, accompanying working mothers will be provided accommodation, with their mothers.
● Working mothers may also avail of the services of the Day Care Centre

2 One Stop Centre One Stop Centres (OSC) are intended to support women affected by violence, in private and public spaces, within the family, community and at the workplace. Women facing physical, sexual, emotional, psychological and economic abuse, irrespective of age, class, caste, education status, marital status, race and culture will be facilitated with support and redressal. Aggrieved women facing any kind of violence due to attempted sexual harassment, sexual assault, domestic violence, trafficking, honour-related crimes, acid attacks or witch-hunting who have reached out or been referred to the OSC will be provided with specialized services. Women under 18- In case girls under 18 years of age are referred to the Centre, they will also be served in coordination with authorities/institutions established under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 and Protection of Children from Sexual Offence Act, 2012. 3 Indian Community Welfare Fund The "Indian Community Welfare Fund (ICWF)" is a program run by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) to help and support Indians living overseas when they face difficult situations. This fund has played a crucial role in evacuating Indian citizens from places with conflicts or natural disasters. Now, it's available at all Indian Missions and Posts abroad. The guidelines for ICWF have been updated to provide more comprehensive support and offer a wider range of help to Indian citizens living abroad. This will allow Indian missions and posts to quickly respond to requests for assistance from Overseas Indian nationals. 4 Babu Jagjivan Ram Chhatrawas Yojna This scheme aims to build hostels to help students from Scheduled Castes (SC) access good education. These hostels are especially helpful for students from rural and remote areas. While hostels for SC girls have been available since the Third Five Year Plan (1961-66), hostels for SC boys started in 1989-90. Girls' hostels will receive full financial support from the central government.
